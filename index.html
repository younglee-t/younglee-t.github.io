Biography

I completed a PhD from the London School of Economics, where my thesis looked at some problems in entropy minimizers for general stochastic processes.

I then worked for the finance industry in London for about 5 years.

After this, I took up a position as a Researcher in the Machine Learning Group at NICTA in 2015, where I worked on inference for stochastic processes.

Starting December 2017, I am a Senior Research Fellow at the NUS.

Research Interests

Semi-martingales, stochastic processes, and (Bayesian) inference thereof
